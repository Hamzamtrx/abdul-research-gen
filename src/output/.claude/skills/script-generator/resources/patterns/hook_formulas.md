# Hook Formulas - Extracted from 10/10 Winners

This file contains fill-in-the-blank hook templates extracted from proven winning scripts. Use these formulas to create scroll-stopping hooks that match the 10/10 standard.

---

## HOOK QUALITY SCALE

**3/10 - NEVER USE:**
- "What's a good [product category] gift?"
- "I found this great [product]"
- Generic, predictable, boring

**7/10 - NOT GOOD ENOUGH:**
- "What [product] gets compliments?"
- "This doctor revealed a secret"
- Somewhat interesting but lacks specificity/extremity

**10/10 - THIS IS THE STANDARD:**
- Unexpected + Visceral + Specific + Outrageous but believable
- Creates "wait, WHAT?" reaction
- Would YOU stop scrolling for this?

## FORMULA CATEGORY 1: SYMPTOM STACKING

### Formula 1A: The Question Stack
```
Did you know that if you [specific symptom 1], [specific symptom 2], [specific symptom 3]... you might have [underlying condition]?

Maybe you've also been getting [unexpected symptom 4], [unexpected symptom 5], or [stubborn problem that won't go away].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Did you know that if you're not hungry in the morning, wake up at 3 a.m. to pee, feel constant fatigue... you might have too much cortisol?"
- "Ladies, did you know that if your farts stink really bad, if you're tired and have no motivation, if you're struggling with hair loss... you might have too much cortisol?"

**When to use**: Supplements, health products, wellness
**Why it works**: Lists 3-4 symptoms that make listener think "wait, that's me!"
**Key**: Symptoms must be visceral and specific, not vague

**Quality checklist**:
✅ At least 3 specific symptoms
✅ Mix of embarrassing + common symptoms
✅ Unexpected connection revealed
✅ "You might have X" creates curiosity

---

### Formula 1B: The Taboo Opener
```
[Gender/Demographic], did you know that if [embarrassing/taboo symptom], [symptom 2], [symptom 3]... you might have [condition]?
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Ladies, did you know that if your farts stink really bad, if you're tired and have no motivation..."

**When to use**: When you want pattern interrupt through shock
**Why it works**: Unexpected, visceral, taboo topics stop scrolling
**Key**: Must be relatable despite being embarrassing

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Immediate gender/demographic targeting
✅ Taboo symptom feels real, not offensive
✅ Followed by more "acceptable" symptoms
✅ Creates "oh my god, finally someone said it" feeling

## FORMULA CATEGORY 2: TRANSFORMATION TEASERS

### Formula 2A: Before/After Time Contrast
```
[Timeframe] ago, I was [emotional low point with specific detail]. [Shorter timeframe] later, I was [dramatic transformation]. But I still don't understand how it actually worked.
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Six months ago, I was crying at my wedding dress fitting because the seamstress couldn't zip me in. Four months later, I was a size 4. But I still don't understand how it actually worked."

**When to use**: Personal transformation stories, UGC
**Why it works**: Creates mystery (dramatic result + confusion = must keep watching)
**Key**: Specific details make it believable

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Specific timeframes (not vague "a while ago")
✅ Emotional moment described vividly
✅ Measurable transformation (size 4, 50 pounds, etc.)
✅ "Mystery" element keeps them watching

---

### Formula 2B: Bold Claim + Stack
```
[Unexpected solution] have changed my life

I couldn't [problem] for [timeframe], now [transformation] and kept it off.

[Another area] was [negative state] for [timeframe], now [transformation compared to prime years].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Beef organs have changed my life. I couldn't lose weight for years, now I've lost 50 pounds and kept it off. My libido disappeared for 13 years, now it's more active than it was in my 20s."

**When to use**: Personal testimonial UGC
**Why it works**: Stacks multiple transformations = comprehensive life change
**Key**: "Kept it off" or "compared to prime years" adds credibility

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Bold opening claim
✅ At least 2-3 transformation areas
✅ Specific numbers and timeframes
✅ Each transformation is significant

## FORMULA CATEGORY 3: CHALLENGE/QUALIFICATION

### Formula 3A: The Barrier Entry
```
If you can't [masculine achievement marker/specific benchmark], keep watching.
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "If you can't bench two plates, keep watching."

**When to use**: Masculine/fitness products, performance optimization
**Why it works**: Challenges ego, creates immediate relevance test
**Key**: Benchmark must be aspirational but attainable

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Specific, measurable benchmark
✅ Aspirational for target audience
✅ Creates "I need to fix this" or "I want to achieve this" feeling
✅ Under 10 words

---

### Formula 3B: The Reframe Series
```
You don't have [medical diagnosis], you're [missing nutrient].
You don't have [condition 2], you're [missing nutrient 2].
And if your [body function] is [crude metaphor], you need [nutrient 3].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "You don't have low T, you're low on zinc. You don't have insomnia, you're missing magnesium. And if your meat's clocking out when it should be clocking in, you need boron."

**When to use**: Masculine health products, men's supplements
**Why it works**: Rapid-fire reframes medical diagnoses as simple deficiencies
**Key**: Speed and metaphor use create momentum

**Quality checklist**:
✅ At least 3 rapid reframes
✅ Final one uses memorable metaphor
✅ Removes "broken" shame, replaces with "depleted"
✅ Each takes 2-3 seconds max

## FORMULA CATEGORY 4: EMERGENCY/CRISIS FRAMES

### Formula 4A: The Emergency Broadcast
```
Emergency broadcast: [Target audience] is [shocking claim with specific number/percentage]. And [they have no idea/you don't realize it].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Emergency broadcast: Modern man is operating at 40% capacity. And he has no idea."

**When to use**: When you want maximum urgency
**Why it works**: "Emergency" grabs attention, specific stat validates
**Key**: Must be backed by observable evidence immediately after

**Quality checklist**:
✅ "Emergency" or similar urgency word
✅ Specific percentage or number
✅ "And they have no idea" creates information gap
✅ Followed by proof points within 10 seconds

---

### Formula 4B: The Observational Alarm
```
[Observable fact about everyone around them]. And [specific percentage/fraction] have no idea [root cause].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Half your office needs three coffees just to think straight. That 3pm crash hitting everyone? It's their cells giving up."

**When to use**: When problem is universal but root cause is hidden
**Why it works**: Makes them look at their environment and see the problem
**Key**: Must be immediately observable by listener

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Observable behavior anyone can verify
✅ Specific numbers (half, 3 coffees, 3pm)
✅ Root cause reveal creates "aha" moment
✅ Creates "oh shit, that's me" recognition

## FORMULA CATEGORY 5: CONSPIRACY/SYSTEM BLAME

### Formula 5A: The Intentional Harm Frame
```
They put [harmful ingredient] in your [everyday item] so you won't [resist/perform].

[Harmful ingredient 2] in your [everyday item 2] so you're too [depleted state] to [basic life function].

But the biggest scam? They let [resource] die so you'd never [reach potential].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "They put fluoride in your water so you won't fight back when they replace you. Seed oils in your food so you're too tired to keep up with your own kids. But the biggest scam? They let the soil die so you'd never reach full power."

**When to use**: Masculine products, anti-establishment positioning
**Why it works**: Creates clear villain, personal stakes, calls to defiance
**Key**: Must escalate to "biggest scam" payoff

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Clear "they" (system, industry, powers)
✅ "So you won't/can't" shows intent
✅ Personal stakes (your kids, your power)
✅ Escalates to biggest reveal

---

### Formula 5B: The Generational Decline
```
Your [symbol of modern dependency] has [large number]. Your [ancestor] had none.

[He/She] [ate/used] [natural item] with [X%] more [quality] than yours and [accomplished impressive feat] through [historical hardship].
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Your medicine cabinet has 12 bottles. Your great-grandfather had none. He ate vegetables with 40% more minerals than yours and raised a family through the Depression."

**When to use**: When you want to create shame about modern weakness
**Why it works**: Generational contrast + historical hardship = "we've declined"
**Key**: Ancestor must have faced HARDER life with LESS help

**Quality checklist**:
✅ Specific number (12 bottles vs. none)
✅ Ancestor comparison (grandfather, great-grandfather)
✅ Verifiable claim (40% more minerals)
✅ Impressive feat during hardship (Depression, war, etc.)

## FORMULA CATEGORY 6: DIALOGUE OPENERS

### Formula 6A: The Outrageous Claim Challenge
```
HOST 1: Wait, hold the phone. You're saying that [target audience] will [bold benefit 1], [bold benefit 2], and [bold benefit 3] if they [unexpected action]????

HOST 2: [Exasperated response] I don't know how many times I need to explain this...
```

**Examples from winners:**
- "Wait, hold the phone. You're saying that all women aged over 30 will have higher energy, reset their hormones, and have higher libido if they eat beef organs????"

**When to use**: Podcast format, two-host conversations
**Why it works**: Skeptic voices audience objections, creates curiosity about answer
**Key**: Benefits must sound too good to be true

**Quality checklist**:
✅ "Wait, hold the phone" or similar interruption
✅ At least 3 bold benefits listed
✅ Unexpected/weird solution mentioned
✅ Multiple question marks for emphasis
✅ Host 2 shows mild frustration (signals expertise)

**Examples from winners:**

✅ Friend's reaction shows it's unexpected
✅ Natural laugh/conversational flow
✅ "Let me tell the story" promises details coming

## HOOK MIXING STRATEGIES

### Strategy 1: Hook + Expansion
Start with short hook (3-5 seconds), then expand with more symptoms/details:
```
[Bold opening hook]
[Expand with 2-3 more related points]
[Connect to underlying condition]
```

### Strategy 2: Hook + Contradiction
Create curiosity through apparent contradiction:
```
[Dramatic transformation statement]
But [thing that makes it confusing/mysterious]
My only real change was [unexpected solution]
```

### Strategy 3: Hook + Question Cascade
Start with one question, cascade into more:
```
[Initial question with symptom]
Maybe you've also been getting [symptom 2]?
And that [symptom 3]?
[Reveal connection]
```

## HOOK VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Before accepting ANY hook, ask:

**The Scroll Test:**
- [ ] Would YOU stop scrolling for this?
- [ ] Does it create "wait, WHAT?" reaction?
- [ ] Is it at least 8/10?

**The Specificity Test:**
- [ ] Are details specific (not vague)?
- [ ] Does it name concrete things (numbers, places, feelings)?
- [ ] Could you visualize this scenario?

**The Believability Test:**
- [ ] Is it exaggerated but not fake?
- [ ] Could this reasonably happen?
- [ ] Does it pass the "sounds real" test?

**The Relevance Test:**
- [ ] Will target audience identify immediately?
- [ ] Does it hit a pain point or desire?
- [ ] Is it surprising for THIS audience?

**The Extreme Test:**
- [ ] Is there a MORE extreme version?
- [ ] Have you pushed it far enough?
- [ ] Is it memorable?

**If you answer "no" or "maybe" to any → REWRITE**

## COMMON HOOK MISTAKES TO AVOID

**❌ Too Vague:**
- "I found something that changed my life"
- **Fix:** "Beef organs changed my life - I lost 50 pounds and my libido returned after 13 years"

**❌ Not Specific Enough:**
- "If you're struggling with energy..."
- **Fix:** "If you need three coffees just to think straight and still crash at 3pm..."

**❌ No Stakes:**
- "Have you heard of this supplement?"
- **Fix:** "Your great-grandfather had zero supplements. You have 12 bottles. He raised a family through the Depression."

**❌ Too Obvious:**
- "Want to feel better? Try this!"
- **Fix:** "Emergency broadcast: Modern man is operating at 40% capacity. And he has no idea."

**❌ Not Extreme Enough:**
- "This product helped me lose weight"
- **Fix:** "I was crying at my wedding dress fitting. Four months later, I was a size 4."

## FINAL HOOK QUALITY STANDARD

**A 10/10 hook must:**
1. ✅ Stop scroll in first 3 seconds
2. ✅ Create "wait, WHAT?" reaction
3. ✅ Be specific and visceral
4. ✅ Be outrageous but believable
5. ✅ Make YOU want to keep watching
6. ✅ Hit pain point or desire immediately
7. ✅ Create information gap ("how?")
8. ✅ Be memorable 24 hours later

**If it doesn't hit ALL 8 → it's not 10/10 → REWRITE**
